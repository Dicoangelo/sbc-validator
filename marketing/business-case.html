The wedge · why now

A cryptographic deadline that hard-stops voice.

Teams Direct Routing runs entirely on mutual TLS between the customer's SBC and Microsoft's SIP proxy. In 2026 Microsoft retired the legacy roots and is enforcing serverAuth-only certificates. An SBC whose trust store or cert is not updated does not degrade. It hard-stops.

The failure mode

Instant TLS handshake rejection, SIP 408 timeouts, complete inbound and outbound outage, with nothing in Teams pointing at the certificate. The classic "scream test": you find out from users, not from a dashboard. Tracked as Microsoft Message Center MC1235747.

The cost of getting it wrong

It is already happening, in production.

Voipcloud
P1 · thousands of users

Microsoft stopped accepting calls routed through their SBCs: a temporally valid but untrusted root presented at the mTLS handshake. Full outage.

CallTower
Silent blackhole

Unpatched SBCs stopped sending SIP OPTIONS pings; Microsoft auto-deactivated the Direct Routing domain and blackholed every voice route, with no SIP 503 to warn anyone.

FusionConnect
One-way audio

Signaling succeeded, but media relays presented new DigiCert certs and silently dropped outbound audio (SRTP mismatch). The hardest failure to diagnose.

Practitioners rate SBC config complexity 6–8 out of 10. Teams learn about trunk outages from user complaints and LinkedIn posts. There is no cross-vendor, pre-deployment tool to catch this before it ships.

What it is

Validate the config before the call, not the call after the outage.

SBC-AutoOps parses any vendor's config into one normalized model and runs eight validation domains over it. Deterministic verdicts, not LLM guesses. It runs inside your environment, air-gapped; raw configs never leave.

VALIDATE

Catch it from config

Eight domains: TLS/CA roots and EKU and chain, SRTP, NAT/one-way-audio, codec, routing, interop, security/ACL. The 2026 wedge is domain C.

SIMULATE

Predict the call

Models the call as a chain (TLS → SIP → SDP → media), predicts how far it gets, names the user-visible symptom, and renders the SIP ladder up to the failure.

EXPLAIN

Diagnose a capture

Reconstructs the SIP ladder from a packet capture, detects one-way audio and TLS alerts, and maps each failure back to the config fix.

Four real vendor parsers on one model: AudioCodes, Cisco CUBE, Ribbon, Oracle Acme. Plus HA-drift detection, fleet readiness, and a CI/CD gate.
