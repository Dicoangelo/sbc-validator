The wedge · why now

A cryptographic deadline that hard-stops voice.

Teams Direct Routing runs entirely on mutual TLS between your SBC and Microsoft's SIP proxy. In 2026, Microsoft retires the legacy roots and enforces serverAuth-only certificates. An SBC whose trust store or cert is not updated does not degrade gracefully. It stops, completely.

The failure mode

Instant TLS handshake rejection. SIP 408 timeouts. Total inbound and outbound outage. And nothing in Teams points at the certificate: you find out from users, not from a dashboard. Engineers call it the "scream test." Tracked as Microsoft Message Center MC1235747.

How it works

Validate the config before the call, not the call after the outage.

Four real vendor parsers normalize any config into one model, and the engine runs validation domains over it. Deterministic verdicts, not LLM guesses. It runs inside your environment, fully air-gapped.

Catch it from config

Eight domains: syntax, interop, the TLS/CA wedge with SRTP and trust-anchor chains, NAT and one-way audio, codec, topology leak, routing, security. The 2026 failure is caught before a single packet is sent.

02 · Simulate

Predict the call

Models the call as a chain (TLS, then SIP, then SDP, then media), predicts exactly how far it gets, names the user-visible symptom, and renders the SIP ladder up to the point of failure.

03 · Explain

Reconstructs the SIP ladder from a packet capture, detects one-way audio and TLS alerts, and maps each failure back to the config change that fixes it.
